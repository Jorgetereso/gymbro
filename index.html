Periodization

8 weeks. Each phase builds on the last. Don't skip the deload.
Weeks 1-2 — Foundation
Dial in form, log every weight. RPE 7-8 (2-3 reps in the tank). Same volume both weeks — this is your baseline. If you're sore for 4 days, you went too hard.
Weeks 3-4 — Volume Build
+1 set on every main lift. Same weights or small bumps. RPE 7-8. Your work capacity grows now — extra sets, not extra weight.
Weeks 5-6 — Intensity Peak
Drop 1 set back, push weight up. Top sets at RPE 8-9 (1 rep in the tank on the last set). This is where strength actually goes up.
Week 7 — Overreach
Highest weekly volume + intensity. Will feel rough. Sleep 8+ hours. Eat. This is by design.
Week 8 — Deload + Measure
50-60% of normal volume, easy weights. Body supercompensates. Take photos + measurements + redo bloodwork. Compare to Day 1.

The Plan

Why This Plan Is Built This Way

You're 1.80m / 73.5 kg with abs visible after 3 years of training. You're not fat — you're a lean intermediate who wants more muscle. So this is a lean bulk, not a cut. The honest 8-week target is +1.5-2 kg of mostly muscle (~75 kg), abs still visible, measurably bigger arms / shoulders / chest / back.

You train best when you don't have to leave the house. So this is a 4-day home program built around your current setup: 2× 10 kg DBs, 5 kg medicine ball, TRX, doorway pull-up bar. At this load you can't bully your legs with pure kilos — so we replace missing weight with tempo, pauses, and single-leg work. Same growth signal, lower load. As you upgrade gear (see Things To Buy), the program scales with you.

Honest Reality Check

"Ripped in 2 months" is a marketing phrase. You're already lean. What you really want — more muscle while keeping the abs — is a lean bulk, and the natural ceiling for an intermediate over 8 weeks is about 1.5-2 kg of muscle.

Anyone — Instagram, YouTube, AI — promising 5+ kg of muscle in 60 days naturally is lying or on PEDs. Trust the boring math: progressive overload + 0.3 kg/week bodyweight gain + 160g protein + sleep.

Home Gym — What To Buy & When

Tier 1 = this week. Tier 2 = once you're consistent. Tier 3 = the unlock.
YOU ALREADY HAVE
2× 10 kg DBs • 5 kg medicine ball • Doorway pull-up bar • TRX

TIER 1 — This week (~$300)

Adjustable dumbbells 5-25 kg / each
The single biggest upgrade. PowerBlocks, Bowflex SelectTech 552, or budget alternative. Your 10 kg pair caps every push/pull movement. Without this the program plateaus by week 3. ~$200-400.
Resistance band set (light / med / heavy loops)
Cheap (~$30), fills gaps for face pulls, pull-aparts, banded squats, warm-ups.
Training mat (~$30)
Floor press, glute bridges, planks, ab work. Not optional with hardwood/tile floors.

TIER 2 — Month 1-2 (~$700)

Pull the trigger once you've shown up 12+ sessions in a row. Don't buy in advance.
Olympic barbell (20 kg) + 60-80 kg of plates
Real bar with rotating sleeves. Lets you squat / deadlift / row at proper weight. ~$300-500.
Squat stands or half-rack with safety arms
Non-negotiable for heavy squat/bench. Safeties save you when a rep dies. ~$200-500.
Adjustable bench (flat/incline/decline)
Bench press + incline DB + supported rows. ~$150-300.

TIER 3 — Later (when you're a regular)

More plates (up to 120+ kg total)
Once your squat passes ~80 kg you'll need more.
Pulley / lat pulldown attachment for rack
Adds cable rows, lat pulldowns, tricep pushdowns — the home gym's missing piece. ~$150.
Dip bars / parallettes
Dips = king for chest + tricep. ~$50-100.

CHEAP & ALWAYS USEFUL

L-Citrulline malate powder
6-8 g, 30 min pre-workout. Better pumps, more reps.
Caffeine pills (200 mg) or plain coffee
Pre-workout. Don't take after 16:00.
Electrolyte mix (LMNT or DIY)
Sodium + potassium on hard training days.
Body weight scale
Same time every morning. Weekly average is what matters.
Measuring tape
Chest, arms, waist, thighs. Week 1, 4, 8.
